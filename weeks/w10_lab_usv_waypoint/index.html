Lab 3 — USV Waypoint Mission Challenge

Week 10 · Spring 2026

Overview

  • Use the stabilization layer you tuned in Lab 2 as the foundation for autonomously following a sequence of waypoints with ArduRover’s guidance and navigation stack.
  • More exploratory than Lab 1 and Lab 2: we provide the framework, the goal, and a baseline configuration; you are welcome to use all the tools at your disposal to iterate and improve on that baseline. (This is an authentic exploration of the autopilot’s capabilities. We don’t know how it is going to turn out!)
  • The feedback concepts from Lab 2 (proportional error, feedforward, transient performance) reappear at the guidance layer. We will use that to motivate what’s happening; a full treatment of guidance and navigation is beyond the scope of this class.

Waypoint Mission Challenge

  • Minimize course time for a fixed mission of 3–5 waypoints.
  • Course time is defined as
    • Start: the first moment the USV enters the acceptance threshold around waypoint 1.
    • End: the first moment the USV enters the acceptance threshold around the final waypoint.
  • Two measurements per run:
    • Stopwatch + Mission Planner display, recorded by the team in the field (primary measurement).
    • Log-derived estimate from post-processing (target — exact scoring approach TBD).

Description

  • In Lab 2 each stabilization loop had the same internal structure: setpoint → error → PID (with feedforward) → actuator.
  • The guidance layer applies the same pattern, one level up:
    • Setpoint = next waypoint.
    • Error = position relative to the waypoint (a vector, but treated similarly to a scalar error in the proportional sense).
    • “Controller output” = a speed and yaw-rate command sent down to the stabilization layer — i.e. the same THR_DesSpeed and STER_DesTurnRate channels you logged in Lab 2.
  • In other words: Lab 3’s guidance layer is a proportional position controller wrapped around your Lab-2 PIDs.
  • See Guidance & Navigation — How the Waypoint Mission Works for the full walk-through.

Out of scope for this lab:

  • The internals of ArduRover’s trajectory generator (S-curve).
  • Tuning the position controller (PSC_* gains).
  • The EKF and state estimation.

These exist in the system and you will see their footprints in the logs, but they are not subjects you are expected to tune or analyze.

Schedule

Lab 3 runs in a single week with two field sessions. Suggested rhythm:

  • Session 1 — Shakedown. Load Lab-2 tuned gains + baseline G&N parameters. Run the standard course at least once, record stopwatch time, and capture the log. Identify what’s working and what’s not (saturation, slow corners, missed acceptance threshold, parameter values you suspect, etc.) so you have a concrete agenda for session 2.
  • Session 2 — Iterate and capture best attempt. Adjust whatever the shakedown surfaced — your Lab-2 PID gains, the tunable G&N handles (WP_SPEED, WP_RADIUS, WP_OVERSHOOT), or your team’s mission-execution technique — and run the course enough times to lock in a result you are willing to submit.

Reading

Before session 1:

  • Guidance & Navigation — How the Waypoint Mission Works — companion page; the conceptual frame for everything in this lab.
  • Autopilot → Stabilization Layer — Lab 2 reference. Re-skim because the position controller hands its outputs to the channels you already tuned.
  • ArduRover → Rover Tuning Navigation — parameter handbook for the G&N layer.

Procedure

Procedure — pre-lab checklist, uploading the mission via Mission Planner, in-water safety, stopwatch protocol, and log capture.

Deliverable

One PowerPoint slide per submitted run, containing:

  • Trajectory plot from the log, waypoints overlaid on the actual path.
  • Course time, stopwatch and log-derived, side by side.

Minimum: one slide for a clean run of the standard course. Optional: extra slides for variations you tried (different WP_SPEED, different Lab-2 gain set, etc.).
